  1. Unlike American ice cream, gelato—a frozen dessert from Italy—is made with whole milk rather than cream and contains no eggs
  2. As a result, this slow-churned treat takes on a denser, almost stretchy consistency.
  3. Gelato is available in a wide range of flavors, but some popular picks are cream, cioccolato (chocolate), stracciatella (vanilla with chocolate flecks) and pistacchio
Sherbet

₹300

  1. Sherbet is a frozen treat made from fruit purée, plus a scant amount of dairy.
  2. Although sherbet is always made with some form of dairy—be it milk, cream or even buttermilk—the resulting dessert is distinct from ice cream in that it contains only 1 to 2 percent milkfat
  3. As such, sherbet is only slightly creamy and considerably lighter than good old fashioned ice cream.

  1. This traditional Indian ice cream is made by slowly heating sweetened milk until the sugar caramelizes and the milk condenses; the mixture is then frozen .
  2. As a result, kulfi is denser and creamier than American ice cream, with a custard-like quality that lends itself well to being served on a stick.
